A little bit about me.

My journey into molecular biology started in 2010, when I was studying medical genetics during my 4th year in Saint Petersburg State Medical Academy. I quickly became fascinated by the concept of polymerase chain reaction, its simplicity and endless possibilities. Experience with sequencing and fluorescence in situ hybridization followed soon.

That fascination opened a door that could never be shut. Since then I have developed an interest in cutting edge biomedical research. I have implemented PCR as a method of detecting infectious agents in the clinical laboratory where I was employed in 2016-2018. I also enjoyed sharing my experience and knowledge with students while I was teaching at the local medical institute.
